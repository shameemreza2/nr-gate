OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing)	<b>正交频分复用</b><br><br>LTE and 5G NR both use OFDM in the downlink to divide the channel into orthogonal subcarriers.	telecom RF
subcarrier spacing (SCS)	<b>子载波间隔</b><br><br>5G NR supports multiple SCS values (15, 30, 60, 120, 240 kHz) to serve different deployment scenarios.	telecom RF
SINR (Signal-to-Interference-plus-Noise Ratio)	<b>信干噪比</b><br><br>A UE reports SINR to the gNB to guide modulation and coding scheme selection.	telecom RF
path loss	<b>路径损耗</b><br><br>Free-space path loss increases with the square of distance and the square of carrier frequency.	telecom RF
fading	<b>衰落</b><br><br>Multipath fading causes rapid fluctuations in received signal power in urban environments.	telecom RF
MIMO (Multiple-Input Multiple-Output)	<b>多输入多输出</b><br><br>LTE supports 4×4 MIMO in the downlink, and 5G NR scales to massive MIMO with up to 64 transmit antennas.	telecom RF
beamforming	<b>波束赋形</b><br><br>Beamforming directs the transmitted energy toward the intended UE, increasing coverage and reducing interference.	telecom RF
antenna gain	<b>天线增益</b><br><br>A sector antenna with 18 dBi gain concentrates energy in a specific direction, extending coverage range.	telecom RF
QAM (Quadrature Amplitude Modulation)	<b>正交幅度调制</b><br><br>64-QAM encodes 6 bits per symbol; 5G NR supports up to 256-QAM for high-throughput links in good channel conditions.	telecom RF
cyclic prefix (CP)	<b>循环前缀</b><br><br>The cyclic prefix absorbs inter-symbol interference caused by multipath delay spread, at the cost of overhead.	telecom RF
multipath propagation	<b>多径传播</b><br><br>Multipath propagation causes constructive and destructive interference, leading to frequency-selective fading.	telecom RF
RSRP (Reference Signal Received Power)	<b>参考信号接收功率</b><br><br>RSRP is the primary cell-selection and handover trigger; values range from −44 dBm (excellent) to −140 dBm (cell edge).	telecom RF
CQI (Channel Quality Indicator)	<b>信道质量指示</b><br><br>The UE feeds back a CQI value (0–15) to help the scheduler select the most efficient MCS for current channel conditions.	telecom RF
MCS (Modulation and Coding Scheme)	<b>调制与编码方案</b><br><br>A higher MCS index means higher throughput but requires better SINR; the scheduler selects MCS based on the UE's CQI report.	telecom RF
spectrum efficiency	<b>频谱效率</b><br><br>LTE Release 8 achieves about 16 bits/s/Hz peak spectral efficiency; 5G NR targets over 30 bits/s/Hz.	telecom RF
uplink	<b>上行链路</b><br><br>The uplink carries data from the UE to the base station; LTE uses SC-FDMA in the uplink to reduce peak-to-average power ratio.	telecom RF
downlink	<b>下行链路</b><br><br>The downlink carries data from the base station to the UE; both LTE and 5G NR use OFDM in the downlink.	telecom RF
interference	<b>干扰</b><br><br>Intercell interference is the dominant impairment at the cell edge; ICIC and beamforming are used to mitigate it.	telecom RF
frequency reuse	<b>频率复用</b><br><br>A frequency reuse factor of 1 means every cell uses the entire spectrum; OFDM enables this without the guard bands required by CDMA.	telecom RF
channel bandwidth	<b>信道带宽</b><br><br>LTE supports channel bandwidths of 1.4, 3, 5, 10, 15, and 20 MHz; 5G NR supports up to 400 MHz in FR2.	telecom RF
noise figure	<b>噪声系数</b><br><br>A low-noise-figure LNA at the antenna port is critical for improving uplink sensitivity at the cell edge.	telecom RF
Doppler shift	<b>多普勒频移</b><br><br>At vehicular speeds, Doppler shifts cause inter-carrier interference in OFDM, requiring channel estimation to compensate.	telecom RF
RSRQ (Reference Signal Received Quality)	<b>参考信号接收质量</b><br><br>RSRQ combines signal strength (RSRP) and interference (RSSI), providing a better indicator of signal quality than RSRP alone.	telecom RF
TDD (Time Division Duplex)	<b>时分双工</b><br><br>China's 5G NR deployments in the 2.6 GHz and 3.5 GHz bands use TDD, sharing one frequency for both uplink and downlink.	telecom RF
FDD (Frequency Division Duplex)	<b>频分双工</b><br><br>FDD uses separate uplink and downlink frequencies and is common for LTE deployments in sub-1 GHz bands for wide-area coverage.	telecom RF
eNB (Evolved Node B)	<b>演进型基站</b><br><br>The eNB is the LTE base station that handles radio resource management and connects to the EPC via the S1 interface.	telecom LTE
EPC (Evolved Packet Core)	<b>演进分组核心网</b><br><br>The EPC consists of the MME, SGW, PGW, and HSS, forming the control and user planes of LTE.	telecom LTE
MME (Mobility Management Entity)	<b>移动性管理实体</b><br><br>The MME handles UE attach, authentication, and handover signalling in the LTE control plane.	telecom LTE
S1 interface	<b>S1接口</b><br><br>The S1 interface connects the eNB to the EPC: S1-MME carries control plane signalling, S1-U carries user data.	telecom LTE
X2 interface	<b>X2接口</b><br><br>The X2 interface between adjacent eNBs enables direct handover signalling without routing through the EPC.	telecom LTE
UE (User Equipment)	<b>用户设备</b><br><br>The UE can be a smartphone, IoT sensor, or CPE; in 3GPP specifications it is always referred to as the UE.	telecom LTE
SGW (Serving Gateway)	<b>服务网关</b><br><br>The SGW is the user-plane anchor for LTE; it routes data between the eNB and the PGW, and buffers packets during handover.	telecom LTE
PGW (PDN Gateway)	<b>PDN网关</b><br><br>The PGW connects the EPC to external packet data networks, assigning IP addresses and enforcing QoS policies.	telecom LTE
HSS (Home Subscriber Server)	<b>归属用户服务器</b><br><br>The HSS stores subscriber profiles, authentication credentials, and roaming permissions for every UE in the network.	telecom LTE
PCRF (Policy and Charging Rules Function)	<b>策略与计费规则功能</b><br><br>The PCRF generates policy and charging rules in real time, allowing the operator to prioritise video or throttle heavy users.	telecom LTE
EPS bearer	<b>EPS承载</b><br><br>A default EPS bearer is created at attach; dedicated bearers with stricter QoS are added for services like VoIP or video.	telecom LTE
handover	<b>切换</b><br><br>An intra-frequency LTE handover is triggered by an A3 event when a neighbour cell's RSRP exceeds the serving cell by a configured offset.	telecom LTE
RACH (Random Access Channel)	<b>随机接入信道</b><br><br>The UE uses the RACH procedure to gain uplink synchronisation before it can transmit data; it sends a preamble on the PRACH.	telecom LTE
PDSCH (Physical Downlink Shared Channel)	<b>物理下行共享信道</b><br><br>The PDSCH carries user data and higher-layer control messages from the eNB to the UE in the LTE downlink.	telecom LTE
PUSCH (Physical Uplink Shared Channel)	<b>物理上行共享信道</b><br><br>The PUSCH carries uplink data and control information from the UE to the eNB after the UE has been granted an uplink resource.	telecom LTE
PDCCH (Physical Downlink Control Channel)	<b>物理下行控制信道</b><br><br>The PDCCH carries DCI, which tells the UE which resource blocks are allocated and which MCS to use.	telecom LTE
PCI (Physical Cell Identity)	<b>物理小区标识</b><br><br>Each LTE cell is assigned a unique PCI (0–503) used by UEs for cell identification, synchronisation, and reference signal generation.	telecom LTE
TAC (Tracking Area Code)	<b>跟踪区码</b><br><br>The TAC identifies a tracking area; when a UE moves to a new TA, it performs a TAU procedure to update the network.	telecom LTE
IMSI (International Mobile Subscriber Identity)	<b>国际移动用户识别码</b><br><br>The IMSI is a 15-digit number stored on the SIM; it uniquely identifies the subscriber across all 3GPP networks.	telecom LTE
QoS (Quality of Service)	<b>服务质量</b><br><br>LTE QoS is defined by QCI values (1–9) that specify latency, packet loss, and priority for each type of service.	telecom LTE
carrier aggregation (CA)	<b>载波聚合</b><br><br>LTE-A carrier aggregation can combine up to 5 component carriers, each up to 20 MHz, for a peak downlink of 100 MHz.	telecom LTE
VoLTE (Voice over LTE)	<b>LTE语音</b><br><br>VoLTE carries voice as an IMS application over the LTE data plane, eliminating CSFB and providing HD voice quality.	telecom LTE
DRX (Discontinuous Reception)	<b>非连续接收</b><br><br>DRX reduces UE power consumption by allowing the radio to sleep in predictable off-periods; LTE supports connected-mode and idle-mode DRX.	telecom LTE
CRS (Cell-specific Reference Signal)	<b>小区参考信号</b><br><br>The CRS is transmitted every subframe in LTE, enabling channel estimation; it is replaced by CSI-RS in 5G NR.	telecom LTE
attach procedure	<b>附着流程</b><br><br>The attach procedure authenticates the UE, establishes the default bearer, and assigns an IP address, involving MME, HSS, SGW, and PGW.	telecom LTE
paging	<b>寻呼</b><br><br>When the network needs to reach an idle UE, it sends a paging message on the PDCCH in every cell of the UE's tracking area.	telecom LTE
LTE-A (LTE Advanced)	<b>LTE高级版</b><br><br>LTE-A (Release 10) introduced carrier aggregation, enhanced MIMO, and HetNet features beyond the original LTE specification.	telecom LTE
HARQ (Hybrid Automatic Repeat Request)	<b>混合自动重传请求</b><br><br>HARQ operates at Layer 1, retransmitting failed packets within milliseconds; it is much faster than RLC ARQ at Layer 2.	telecom LTE
resource block (RB)	<b>资源块</b><br><br>One resource block spans 12 subcarriers × 0.5 ms; the LTE scheduler allocates RBs per subframe to each UE.	telecom LTE
timing advance (TA)	<b>时间提前量</b><br><br>The eNB measures the uplink propagation delay and commands a TA value so all UEs' transmissions arrive synchronised at the antenna.	telecom LTE
gNB (Next Generation Node B)	<b>5G基站</b><br><br>The gNB is the 5G NR base station; it connects to the 5GC via the NG interface and to other gNBs via Xn.	telecom 5G
5GC (5G Core)	<b>5G核心网</b><br><br>The 5GC uses a service-based architecture (SBA) with network functions such as AMF, SMF, and UPF.	telecom 5G
SA — Standalone (5G)	<b>独立组网</b><br><br>In SA mode, 5G NR connects directly to the 5GC without relying on an LTE anchor.	telecom 5G
NSA — Non-Standalone (5G)	<b>非独立组网</b><br><br>In NSA mode (Option 3x), the LTE eNB provides the control plane anchor while NR adds capacity in dual connectivity.	telecom 5G
AMF (Access and Mobility Management Function)	<b>接入和移动性管理功能</b><br><br>The AMF replaces the LTE MME, handling registration, connection management, and mobility in the 5GC.	telecom 5G
SMF (Session Management Function)	<b>会话管理功能</b><br><br>The SMF manages PDU session establishment, IP address allocation, and uplink classifier rules in the 5G core.	telecom 5G
UPF (User Plane Function)	<b>用户面功能</b><br><br>The UPF is the user-plane anchor in 5G; it performs packet routing, QoS enforcement, and can be placed at the edge for low latency.	telecom 5G
PCF (Policy Control Function)	<b>策略控制功能</b><br><br>The PCF provides unified policy control for access, mobility, session management, and application influence in the 5GC.	telecom 5G
UDM (Unified Data Management)	<b>统一数据管理</b><br><br>The UDM stores subscriber data and generates authentication credentials; it replaces the HSS role from LTE.	telecom 5G
NRF (Network Repository Function)	<b>网络存储功能</b><br><br>The NRF maintains a registry of all network function instances, enabling service-based discovery and selection within the 5GC.	telecom 5G
O-RAN (Open RAN)	<b>开放无线接入网</b><br><br>O-RAN defines open interfaces between RAN components, enabling multi-vendor deployments and AI-driven optimisation via the RIC.	telecom 5G
CU (Central Unit)	<b>集中单元</b><br><br>The CU hosts the upper RAN layers (PDCP, RRC, SDAP) and can serve multiple DUs, enabling centralised processing.	telecom 5G
DU (Distributed Unit)	<b>分布单元</b><br><br>The DU hosts lower RAN layers (RLC, MAC, lower PHY) and connects to the CU via the F1 interface.	telecom 5G
RU (Radio Unit)	<b>射频单元</b><br><br>The RU performs RF processing and lower-PHY functions; it connects to the DU via the Open Fronthaul interface.	telecom 5G
mmWave (millimetre wave)	<b>毫米波</b><br><br>mmWave frequencies (above 24 GHz) provide multi-Gbps throughput but require dense deployments due to severe path loss and blockage.	telecom 5G
FR1 (Frequency Range 1 — sub-6 GHz)	<b>频率范围1（sub-6G）</b><br><br>FR1 (410 MHz–7.125 GHz) is the primary band for 5G deployments worldwide, balancing coverage and capacity.	telecom 5G
FR2 (Frequency Range 2 — mmWave)	<b>频率范围2（毫米波）</b><br><br>FR2 (24.25–52.6 GHz) offers very large bandwidths for peak throughput but requires line-of-sight or near-LOS conditions.	telecom 5G
massive MIMO	<b>大规模天线阵列</b><br><br>A massive MIMO array with 64 or more antenna elements enables spatial multiplexing of many users simultaneously on the same resource.	telecom 5G
network slicing	<b>网络切片</b><br><br>A network slice is an end-to-end logical network on shared infrastructure, customised for a specific service (eMBB, URLLC, or mMTC).	telecom 5G
MEC (Multi-access Edge Computing)	<b>多接入边缘计算</b><br><br>MEC deploys compute at the edge of the RAN, reducing round-trip latency to below 5 ms for latency-critical applications.	telecom 5G
URLLC (Ultra-Reliable Low-Latency Communication)	<b>超高可靠低时延通信</b><br><br>URLLC targets 1 ms latency and 99.9999% reliability for applications such as industrial automation, remote surgery, and V2X.	telecom 5G
eMBB (enhanced Mobile Broadband)	<b>增强型移动宽带</b><br><br>eMBB delivers peak data rates of 20 Gbps downlink and 10 Gbps uplink, meeting demand for 8K video, VR, and AR.	telecom 5G
mMTC (massive Machine-Type Communication)	<b>大规模机器类通信</b><br><br>mMTC supports up to 1 million connected devices per km², targeting smart city and industrial IoT use cases.	telecom 5G
SBA (Service-Based Architecture)	<b>基于服务的架构</b><br><br>In the SBA, network functions expose services via HTTP/2 APIs, enabling modular deployment and independent scaling of each function.	telecom 5G
Xn interface	<b>Xn接口</b><br><br>The Xn interface connects adjacent gNBs, supporting handover, dual connectivity, and load balancing without involving the core.	telecom 5G
NG interface	<b>NG接口</b><br><br>The NG interface connects the gNB to the AMF (control plane via NG-C) and UPF (user plane via NG-U) in the 5GC.	telecom 5G
SSB (Synchronization Signal Block)	<b>同步信号块</b><br><br>The SSB carries the PSS, SSS, and PBCH, enabling initial cell search and beam measurement; it is transmitted in a burst set.	telecom 5G
BWP (Bandwidth Part)	<b>带宽部分</b><br><br>A BWP is a subset of the carrier bandwidth that a UE is configured to use, enabling power saving on wide 5G NR carriers.	telecom 5G
DCI (Downlink Control Information)	<b>下行控制信息</b><br><br>The DCI is carried on the PDCCH and conveys scheduling grants, MCS, resource allocation, and HARQ information to the UE.	telecom 5G
SRS (Sounding Reference Signal)	<b>探测参考信号</b><br><br>The UE transmits the SRS so the gNB can estimate the uplink channel and inform downlink beamforming based on channel reciprocity.	telecom 5G
dual connectivity	<b>双连接</b><br><br>In EN-DC (LTE–NR dual connectivity), the UE is simultaneously connected to an LTE master node and an NR secondary node.	telecom 5G
beam management	<b>波束管理</b><br><br>Beam management procedures (P1–P3) select and refine the best transmit/receive beam pair between the gNB and UE in mmWave deployments.	telecom 5G
CSI-RS (Channel State Information Reference Signal)	<b>信道状态信息参考信号</b><br><br>The gNB transmits CSI-RS to allow the UE to estimate the channel and report CQI, PMI, and RI for link adaptation.	telecom 5G
NR-U (NR Unlicensed)	<b>NR免授权频谱</b><br><br>NR-U extends 5G NR to unlicensed bands (5 GHz, 6 GHz), using LBT (listen-before-talk) to coexist with Wi-Fi.	telecom 5G
IAB (Integrated Access and Backhaul)	<b>集成接入与回传</b><br><br>IAB enables a gNB to wirelessly backhaul traffic from another gNB, reducing the need for fibre to every 5G small cell site.	telecom 5G
NB-IoT (Narrowband IoT)	<b>窄带物联网</b><br><br>NB-IoT operates in a 180 kHz bandwidth and is deployed in-band, guard-band, or standalone within LTE spectrum.	telecom IoT
eMTC (enhanced Machine-Type Communication / LTE-M)	<b>增强型机器类通信</b><br><br>eMTC supports voice and mobility, making it suitable for wearables and asset trackers alongside NB-IoT.	telecom IoT
PSM (Power Saving Mode)	<b>省电模式</b><br><br>PSM allows an IoT device to enter a deep-sleep state for hours or days, extending battery life to several years.	telecom IoT
eDRX (extended Discontinuous Reception)	<b>扩展非连续接收</b><br><br>eDRX extends the paging cycle to minutes or hours, allowing NB-IoT devices to sleep very long periods and achieve 10-year battery life.	telecom IoT
LPWAN (Low-Power Wide-Area Network)	<b>低功耗广域网</b><br><br>LPWAN technologies include NB-IoT, eMTC, LoRa, and Sigfox; they all trade data rate for range and low power consumption.	telecom IoT
MCL (Maximum Coupling Loss)	<b>最大耦合损耗</b><br><br>NB-IoT targets an MCL of 164 dB, enabling coverage in basements and underground pipes beyond traditional LTE reach.	telecom IoT
coverage enhancement (CE)	<b>覆盖增强</b><br><br>NB-IoT and eMTC use repetition coding to achieve up to 20 dB of coverage enhancement relative to LTE, at the cost of latency.	telecom IoT
MQTT	<b>消息队列遥测传输</b><br><br>MQTT is a lightweight publish-subscribe messaging protocol widely used in IoT systems due to its minimal overhead and QoS support.	telecom IoT
CoAP (Constrained Application Protocol)	<b>受限应用协议</b><br><br>CoAP is a UDP-based protocol designed for constrained IoT devices, translating easily to HTTP and supporting multicast.	telecom IoT
OTA update (Over-the-Air)	<b>空中升级</b><br><br>OTA updates allow firmware to be pushed to millions of IoT devices in the field without physical access, reducing maintenance cost.	telecom IoT
RAI (Release Assistance Indication)	<b>释放辅助指示</b><br><br>RAI is a flag the UE sends to signal it has no more data to transmit, allowing fast radio resource release and power saving.	telecom IoT
single-tone transmission	<b>单音传输</b><br><br>NB-IoT supports single-tone uplink transmission (3.75 kHz or 15 kHz SCS) for the deepest coverage scenarios.	telecom IoT
eSIM (embedded SIM)	<b>嵌入式SIM卡</b><br><br>An eSIM is a programmable SIM embedded in the device, enabling remote provisioning of operator profiles without a physical SIM swap.	telecom IoT
M2M (Machine-to-Machine)	<b>机器对机器通信</b><br><br>M2M communication enables devices to exchange data autonomously; 3GPP's standardisation of M2M evolved into the broader IoT framework.	telecom IoT
smart meter	<b>智能电表</b><br><br>NB-IoT-connected smart meters report energy, water, or gas consumption automatically, enabling real-time billing and grid optimisation.	telecom IoT
asset tracking	<b>资产追踪</b><br><br>GPS/GNSS plus cellular connectivity enables real-time tracking of vehicles, containers, and equipment across wide areas.	telecom IoT
in-band deployment (NB-IoT)	<b>带内部署</b><br><br>In-band NB-IoT reuses one PRB within an existing LTE carrier, enabling operators to deploy IoT without additional spectrum.	telecom IoT
guard-band deployment (NB-IoT)	<b>保护带部署</b><br><br>Guard-band NB-IoT uses the unused guard-band of an LTE carrier, avoiding interference with the main LTE signal.	telecom IoT
standalone deployment (NB-IoT)	<b>独立部署</b><br><br>Standalone NB-IoT uses a dedicated 200 kHz carrier refarmed from GSM spectrum, giving operators maximum flexibility.	telecom IoT
repetition coding	<b>重复编码</b><br><br>NB-IoT can repeat the same data block up to 2,048 times to achieve the deep-coverage MCL target, at the cost of data rate.	telecom IoT
anchor carrier	<b>锚载波</b><br><br>The NB-IoT anchor carrier carries system information and paging; the UE must camp on the anchor before using other NB-IoT carriers.	telecom IoT
NPRACH (Narrowband Physical Random Access Channel)	<b>窄带物理随机接入信道</b><br><br>The NPRACH uses single-tone frequency hopping to provide frequency diversity in deep-coverage NB-IoT deployments.	telecom IoT
device management	<b>设备管理</b><br><br>LWM2M (Lightweight M2M) is the standard protocol for remote device management, firmware update, and configuration of IoT devices.	telecom IoT
FOTA (Firmware Over-The-Air)	<b>固件空中升级</b><br><br>FOTA uses the OTA channel to deliver firmware images to IoT devices; differential FOTA sends only changed bytes to save data.	telecom IoT
battery life	<b>电池寿命</b><br><br>NB-IoT targets 10-year battery life for a 5 Wh cell sending 200 bytes per day, assuming PSM and eDRX are both configured.	telecom IoT
PDCP (Packet Data Convergence Protocol)	<b>分组数据汇聚协议</b><br><br>PDCP performs IP header compression, ciphering, and integrity protection in both LTE and 5G NR.	telecom Protocol
RLC (Radio Link Control)	<b>无线链路控制</b><br><br>RLC provides segmentation, reassembly, and ARQ retransmission between the UE and the base station.	telecom Protocol
MAC — Medium Access Control (telecom)	<b>媒体访问控制</b><br><br>The MAC layer schedules uplink and downlink transmissions and performs HARQ retransmissions.	telecom Protocol
PHY (Physical Layer)	<b>物理层</b><br><br>The PHY handles modulation, coding, HARQ, and resource mapping; specified in 3GPP TS 36.211 (LTE) and TS 38.211 (5G NR).	telecom Protocol
NAS (Non-Access Stratum)	<b>非接入层</b><br><br>The NAS handles mobility management and session management between the UE and the core network (MME in LTE, AMF in 5G).	telecom Protocol
AS (Access Stratum)	<b>接入层</b><br><br>The AS encompasses RRC, PDCP, RLC, MAC, and PHY layers; it handles radio-specific functions between the UE and the base station.	telecom Protocol
ARQ (Automatic Repeat Request)	<b>自动重传请求</b><br><br>RLC ARQ retransmits segments not acknowledged by the receiver; it operates over a longer time scale than HARQ.	telecom Protocol
CRC (Cyclic Redundancy Check)	<b>循环冗余校验</b><br><br>A 24-bit CRC is appended to each transport block; if the CRC fails at the receiver, HARQ triggers a retransmission.	telecom Protocol
channel coding	<b>信道编码</b><br><br>LTE uses turbo codes for data channels; 5G NR uses LDPC for data and polar codes for control channels.	telecom Protocol
LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) code	<b>低密度奇偶校验码</b><br><br>LDPC codes are used in 5G NR for data channel coding (PDSCH/PUSCH), offering near-Shannon-limit performance at high code rates.	telecom Protocol
polar code	<b>极化码</b><br><br>Polar codes are used in 5G NR for control channel coding (PDCCH, PBCH), achieving capacity-achieving performance at short block lengths.	telecom Protocol
turbo code	<b>Turbo码</b><br><br>Turbo codes were the channel code of choice for LTE data channels, replaced by LDPC in 5G NR for improved decoding efficiency.	telecom Protocol
rate matching	<b>速率匹配</b><br><br>Rate matching adjusts the number of coded bits to fit the allocated resource, using puncturing or repetition to match the target code rate.	telecom Protocol
resource element (RE)	<b>资源元素</b><br><br>One RE is one subcarrier × one OFDM symbol; a resource block contains 12 × 14 = 168 REs in LTE normal cyclic prefix.	telecom Protocol
transport block (TB)	<b>传输块</b><br><br>Each TTI the scheduler generates a transport block per UE; its size depends on the MCS and the number of allocated resource blocks.	telecom Protocol
TTI (Transmission Time Interval)	<b>传输时间间隔</b><br><br>In LTE the TTI is 1 ms (one subframe); in 5G NR, mini-slots can reduce the scheduling interval to as little as one OFDM symbol.	telecom Protocol
slot	<b>时隙</b><br><br>In 5G NR, one slot contains 14 OFDM symbols; slot duration varies with SCS (0.5 ms at 30 kHz, 0.25 ms at 60 kHz).	telecom Protocol
subframe	<b>子帧</b><br><br>In LTE, one subframe = 1 ms = 2 slots and is the basic scheduling unit; in 5G NR a 1 ms subframe contains multiple slots.	telecom Protocol
OFDMA (Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiple Access)	<b>正交频分多址</b><br><br>OFDMA assigns different subcarriers to different users; the LTE downlink scheduler allocates resource blocks per subframe.	telecom Protocol
SC-FDMA (Single Carrier FDMA)	<b>单载波频分多址</b><br><br>SC-FDMA (used in the LTE uplink) reduces the peak-to-average power ratio compared with OFDMA, extending UE battery life.	telecom Protocol
