## 8. Risk Register & Standing Rules

1. **Tone debt** — the only thing the 6.5-week pre-departure window must produce is a passed tone gate; vocabulary is recoverable later, fossilized tones are expensive. Bangla-L1 evidence says T2 is your hardest perceptual category — overweight it. [R]
2. **SRS floor = 20 min/day, never breaks** — not during exams, not during thesis week, not during travel. It's the compounding asset; everything else can flex to zero.
3. **Slide rule** — at each phase gate, if cum hours <80% of the lower band, slide the next exam one window. No sleep-deficit cramming; you're carrying a research degree and the base rate for grad-student burnout is already bad. [R]
4. **Surge cap 25 h/wk, deload 1-in-6** during breaks.
5. **English-bubble rule** — the lab will default to English. Engineer against it: Mandarin errand rule from week 1, one Chinese-only social slot/wk, phone UI to Chinese at Month 6.
6. **Certificate ≠ goal drift check (quarterly)** — P2 (pitch) and P3 (deck) are the career assets; if a quarter passes with HSK progress but zero speaking/domain progress, the plan is failing even if hours look fine.
7. **Verify before relying:** HIT-SZ arrival date & free Chinese classes; Shenzhen new-format test windows (book early — new-format seats may be tight in the first cycles); whether the program is 2 or 2.5 years.
